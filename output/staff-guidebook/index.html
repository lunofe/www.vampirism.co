Purpose of this document

This guidebook exists to clearly define staff roles, expectations, procedures and standards. It is meant to ensure fair moderation, consistent decisions, and a safe, enjoyable environment for all players and staff members.

Staff philosophy, core values & requirements

As a staff member, you represent the server at all times. Your actions, tone, and decisions directly affect the community.

Our core values

  • Fairness & Objectivity - Rules apply equally to everyone
  • Respect & Empathy - Treat players as people, not problems
  • Consistency - Similar situations should lead to similar outcomes
  • Transparency (where allowed) - Explain decisions without revealing sensitive information
  • Teamwork - Moderation is a shared effort and responsibility

Requirements for every staff member

  • Be friendly, respectful and approachable
  • Be open-minded and empathetic
  • Be willing to help, even if it's inconvenient
  • Be reliable, responsible and reasonable
  • Show maturity, objectivity and emotional control
  • Stay calm in heated situations
  • Accept criticism and feedback
  • Reflect on mistakes and improve

When you're unsure

  • Consult other staff members
  • Never guess or assume when handling punishments
  • Asking questions is a strength, not a weakness

Staff ranks & goals

Helper

Goal: Help players and learn how staff work
Responsibilities:

  • Help players with basic questions, mostly about the game
  • Observe rule violations and report them
  • De-escalate conflicts
  • Learn staff procedures

Junior Moderator

Goal: Enforce our basic rules
Responsibilities:

  • Handle minor punishments
  • Assist in investigations
  • Escalate uncertain cases to higher staff

Moderator

Goal: Maintain order and fairness
Responsibilities:

  • Handle most rule violations
  • Resolve player conflicts
  • Mentor lower staff
  • Ensure proper documentation of any punishments

Head Moderator

Goal: Lead the moderation team
Responsibilities:

  • Oversee moderation as a whole
  • Handle complex or sensitive cases
  • Review punishments and appeals
  • Ensure consistency in moderation
  • Act as bridge between the "day to day business" and "long term topics"

Admin

Goal: Server integrity and enforcement
Responsibilities:

  • Handle extreme cases and permanent bans
  • Manage staff discipline
  • Assist with technical topics
  • Make final decisions when needed

Manager

Goal: Overall server direction
Responsibilities:

  • Final authority on staff matters
  • Server policy decisions
  • Long-term planning and vision
  • Conflict resolution within staff

Rank changes

Initial trial phase

The Helper rank begins with a two-week trial. During this trial, the helper will be gauged on how they handle situations and their overall behaviour. After successfully completing the trial you will retain the Helper rank.

Promotions

Promotions occur naturally over time, typically during or after the annual wipe. We value consistent, high-quality contributions and helpfulness - these matter more than sheer speed or volume.

Not receiving a promotion does not mean you’re not performing well or doing something wrong. Our rank structure is not a strict ladder to climb; instead, we aim to maintain most staff at the moderator level. Above that, there will be one Head Moderator and one or more Admins, both of which carry additional requirements.

Inactivity

There’s no fixed hourly requirement tied to each rank. If you know you’ll be away (vacation or planned absence), please let the staff team know in advance. Extended inactivity with no clear return, because of college, job pressure or other life circumstances may lead to demotion or placement into retirement.

Demotions

Demotions are uncommon and shouldn’t be a source of shame or worry. They’re used only when a role no longer seems like the right fit, for example if the responsibilities prove overwhelming, if a staff member’s behaviour doesn’t meet our expectations, or also if you no longer have sufficient time to carry out the rank's duties reliably.

Retirement

If you have been inactive for an extended period with no change in sight, or simply have a desire to step down, we will move you to retirement. You’re warmly welcome to return to staff if you later decide you’d like to rejoin.

Handling conflicts between players

General approach

  • Stay neutral and observe, don't judge
  • Listen to both sides
  • De-escalate first
  • Explain the rules clearly
  • Apply punishment only if necessary
  • Document the incident

Important rules

  • Never take sides emotionally
  • Never argue aggressively
  • If emotions rise, pause and separate

Working with personal player data

Why this matters

As a staff member, you are handling personal data belonging to real people and handling them responsibly is not a formality but a legal and ethical responsibility.

Under GDPR, any information that can be linked to an identifiable person counts as personal data. Leaking them leads to immediate staff removal and real-life legal consequences.

The core principle

The first rule of Fight Club is you do not talk about Fight Club.

Or: Anything you know because you're staff, stays inside staff.

It does not matter:

  • How harmless it seems
  • Whether the player is popular or not
  • Whether the person asking is a friend or a well-known player
  • Whether the information is "obvious"
  • Whether you think "everyone already knows"

The "I can neither confirm nor deny" tactic

When asked about any internal information, your default response must be: "I can neither confirm nor deny that."

This response exists for a reason:

  • It does not confirm
  • It does not deny
  • It does not narrow down possibilities
  • It protects both the data and you

Standard punishments

This is a brief overview with examples to help guide responses to rule-breaking. It’s not exhaustive.

Treat critical violations (for example, overt racism in chat) with the strongest immediate action available to you, and promptly notify higher staff so they can escalate the sanction to a permanent ban if appropriate.

For less-clear cases, follow our standard enforcement steps: de-escalate, warn, document the incident, and escalate to higher staff when needed.

Verbal warning

Not every misconduct instantly needs a mute or written warning. A verbal warning is a polite request to stop such minor misconduct (e.g. brief chat disruptions, mild rule breaches).

Say it clearly and calmly, state which behavior must stop, and remind the user of the relevant rule. If the behavior continues, escalate to a mute or written warning.

Mute

  • Minor: 5-30 minutes (repeated caps, spam, advertisements, swearing)
  • Major: 1-24 hours (mild insults and comparable infractions)
  • If an offense would warrant a longer mute, use a written warning or ban instead. A mute can still accompany those.

Written warning

  • Temporary: 7-30 days (length depends on the offense, previous offenses, and whether it’s a repeated offense - if you don’t know what to choose, ask higher staff)
  • Permanent (bad offenses, no insight, attempted deception, cheating/hacking)

Ban

  • Temporary
    • Standard: 30 days (automatic when reaching 3 warns, cheating/hacking)
    • Extended: 60 days (bad offenses, no insight, attempted deception)
  • Permanent (ban evasion, being banned for the second time, extreme cases)

Evidence & documentation

Why proper documentation matters

Documentation exists to ensure that:

  • Punishments are fair, justified, and consistent
  • Decisions can be reviewed later by other staff
  • Appeals can be handled objectively

General principles

When documenting evidence, always aim for:

  • Clarity: keep your grammar & spelling in check and avoid non-trivial abbreviations, proverbs or sayings that aren't common and plain English
  • Completeness: any missing context can completely change how an offense is interpreted, so make sure to include everything necessary to grasp the situation at hand
  • Neutrality: document facts from an observer's perspective, not emotions or assumptions of someone involved

What to document

  1. Description of the incident
    • What happened
    • Which rule(s) were broken
    • If relevant, where and when it occurred
  2. Visual evidence (screenshots & recordings)
    • Take screenshots of:
      • Chat messages
      • Player behavior
      • Rule-breaking actions
    • Ensure:
      • Usernames are visible
      • Messages are not overly cropped
  3. Offender player data
    • Username (exact spelling)
    • UUID
    • HWID
    • IP address
    • Known alt accounts (if relevant)
  4. Other people involved
    • Staff
    • Witnesses
    • Provocateurs
    • Victims
  5. Outcome & actions taken
    • What punishment was applied
    • Duration (if applicable)
    • Who applied it

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Writing documentation hours or days later from memory
  • Cropped screenshots that remove important messages

 

Handling ban appeals

Purpose of appeals

People can grow, change, and deserve second chances.

At the same time, moderation is not infallible. Mistakes can happen due to missing information, misinterpretation of evidence, or human error.

Temporary bans are generally not appealable. The appeal system exists to assess change, not to negotiate punishment duration.

Voting procedure & timeframe

Each appeal has a fixed 7-day voting window. Review the case and cast your vote during this time.

The deadline exists to ensure that players are not left waiting indefinitely and to prevent appeals from being buried or forgotten, which can easily happen in a volunteer-based team without a clear time frame.

Once the deadline is reached, the votes that have been submitted up to that point are considered final. A Head Moderator or higher rank gives the final response and closes the case.

Claims of moderation error

If a player claims the ban was issued incorrectly, the case must be handled carefully and objectively.

  • The appeal must be reviewed by a Head Moderator or higher.
  • All evidence must be re-evaluated.
  • The original staff member involved may provide context but is not taking part in the review.
  • The review must be impartial and thorough.

Acknowledging a mistake is not a weakness. It demonstrates professionalism and integrity. If an error is confirmed, corrective action should be taken promptly.

Evaluation criteria

AI-Generated Appeals

Players may use AI tools for translation and grammar or spelling correction. However, of the appeal appears entirely AI-generated with no personal effort, reflection, or individual voice, it's worthless.

Indicators may include:

  • Very generic phrasing, no specific reference to the case
  • Overly formal or artificial structure, lack of personal detail
  • Abstract or vague statements about “personal growth” without specifics
  • Unusual characters: “ & ” instead of " or — instead of -

Time factor

If a player submits an appeal minutes after being banned, within a few hours or even within a few days, it is likely an emotional reaction rather than genuine reflection.

Personal change and growth requires time. A ban that actually “hurts” is often necessary for reflection to begin and to shift the mindset from regret over being caught to genuine remorse and accountability.

Genuine remorse, reflection & change

The most important factor is whether the player demonstrates authentic accountability. Key questions:

  • Do they clearly acknowledge their rule violation?
  • Do they take responsibility without excuses?
  • Do they reflect on why their behavior was harmful?
  • Does the apology feel genuine, or is it merely regret for being caught?

True remorse means regretting the behavior itself, not regretting the punishment.
